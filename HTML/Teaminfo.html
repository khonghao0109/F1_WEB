Team History

Founded in 1963 by New Zealander Bruce McLaren, the team entered its first Formula 1 race in 1966. McLaren quickly established itself as a competitive force, winning its first Grand Prix in 1968 at the Belgian GP with Bruce McLaren himself at the wheel.

The 1970s saw McLaren's first period of sustained success, with Emerson Fittipaldi and James Hunt winning Drivers' Championships in 1974 and 1976 respectively. The partnership with Marlboro began in 1974, starting one of the longest and most famous sponsorships in F1 history.

The 1980s and early 1990s marked McLaren's golden era. With Ron Dennis at the helm, the team dominated with drivers like Niki Lauda, Alain Prost, and Ayrton Senna. The Honda-powered MP4/4 of 1988 remains one of the most dominant cars in F1 history, winning 15 of 16 races that season.

After a less successful period in the mid-1990s, McLaren returned to championship contention with Mika Häkkinen winning back-to-back titles in 1998 and 1999. The 2000s saw fierce competition with Ferrari, with Lewis Hamilton winning his first championship with McLaren in 2008.

In recent years, McLaren has rebuilt after a difficult period, returning to regular podium contention and race victories with Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri at the wheel, establishing itself once again as one of the most competitive teams on the grid.
